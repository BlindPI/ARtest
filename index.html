First Aid & CPR Training FAQ

First Aid and CPR certifications in Ontario are typically valid for 3 years from the date of completion. After this period, you'll need to take a recertification course to maintain your workplace safety credentials and continue to meet WSIB requirements.

CPR-A covers adult CPR techniques only and is sufficient for most workplace requirements. CPR-C includes adult, child, and infant CPR, ideal for childcare workers. CPR-BLS (Basic Life Support) is designed for healthcare professionals in Ontario and includes additional skills like two-rescuer CPR, bag-valve mask techniques, and more complex emergency scenarios.

Yes, we offer on-site first aid and CPR training for businesses, schools, and organizations throughout Barrie, Simcoe County, and all of Ontario. We bring all necessary emergency equipment and materials to your location and can tailor the training to your specific workplace safety requirements for WSIB compliance.

WSIB (Workplace Safety and Insurance Board) approval means our first aid courses meet the strict training standards set by Ontario's workplace safety authority. This ensures that the emergency training satisfies workplace compliance requirements and follows recognized provincial standards for first aid and emergency response training in all Ontario workplaces.

Please bring a valid photo ID, comfortable clothing that allows for movement during practical first aid exercises, and any required pre-course materials if applicable.
